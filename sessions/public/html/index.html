    For many decades, a myriad of scientists, astrophysicists, and sci-fi fans have been debating not only the possibility of terraforming Mars – changing the surface and atmosphere of Mars to make it habitable for humans – but also the ethics of doing so. They break down roughly into three groups – the advocates, the naysayers, and the centrists.
