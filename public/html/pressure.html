    Because Mars’s atmosphere is 100 times thinner than ours, their air pressure Is also low – as little as 1/1000th of that of Earth. For comparison, the pressure on Mt. Everest is 1/3rd that at sea level. There is so little pressure on Mars that there is not enough to prevent water from exploding from below the surface and boiling away, nor enough to keep your body’s fluids from boiling as well. Perhaps less damaging but still daunting would be that the water in your lungs, tears and saliva would evaporate immediately.

    "Pic of explosions of water from beneath the surface"

    Long-term survival would require thickening the atmosphere of Mars, increasing the pressure in order to keep the heat from escaping and water – including our bodily fluids – from boiling. Margarita Marinova has suggested that we might be able to do this by using PFC’s (perflourocarbons) – essentially creating a greenhouse effect on Mars – exactly what we have done – to our detriment – on Earth.

    Other suggestions on increasing the atmospheric pressure on Mars have been to launch a satellite that would point a giant mirror on its polar ice caps, converting the CO2 to a gas. Or using solar panels to convert the CO2 to carbon and oxygen.
